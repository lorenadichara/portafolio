I had always love drawing, connecting with my environment and the people around me. I don’t always perceive drawing as an artistic tool rather as a vibrant pause that returns my scattered mind to the tangible. Since my thoughts tend to be all over the place I developed a close and tender relationship with my body and it’s usually the tool I rely on the most to start my creative process. Listening to it, its desires and impulses gives me queue on what to do or how to approach things in an organic way.

What I love the most about my days are the little moments that bring me peace, such as walking my dogs at night as I connect with nature and enjoy the chill breeze that hugs me, or a late night meditation followed by a blind contour drawing session or journaling; this notes and loose drawings tend to give me insight of how I’m feeling, and later on serve me as guide and inspiration for future projects.
